Unlike most owls, the Short-eared Owl is often active during the day as well, especially in the late afternoon. Its piercing yellow eyes scan the low vegetation of the salt pans.

#### **Distribution and Habitat**

**Global Distribution**
Holarctic and South America.

**Preferred Habitat**
Open wetlands, peat bogs, salt pans, coastal dunes, grasslands.

**Presence in Sicily**
Wintering and migratory. Irregular breeding possible.

**Status in the Salt Pans**
Regular winter visitor. It is observed at dusk flying low and silently over the banks rich in halophytic vegetation.

**Morphological Characteristics**
Medium-sized owl (34-42 cm).
Pale yellow-ochre plumage streaked with brown.
Round head with **very small ear tufts** (often invisible).
**Lemon-yellow eyes** outlined in black ("mask" effect).

**Behavior**
Hunts in low-altitude flight ("quartering"), gliding with wings in a soft "V". Often perches on the ground.

**Diet**
Small mammals (voles, mice), occasionally birds.

**Reproduction**
Nests on the ground (a rare case among nocturnal raptors) hidden among tall grass or rushes.

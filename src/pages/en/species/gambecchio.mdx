The Little Stint is one of the smallest waders. During migration and winter, they form frantic flocks that move in unison across the muddy expanses of the salt pans.

#### **Distribution and Habitat**

**Global Distribution**
Breeds in the far north (Siberian and Scandinavian Arctic tundra). Migrates very long distances to winter in Africa and the Mediterranean.

**Preferred Habitat**
Coastal wetlands, estuaries, salt pans with mudflats exposed by tide or evaporation.

**Presence in Sicily**
Common migrant and winter visitor.

**Status in the Salt Pans**
Very numerous in winter and during passages. Observed in active groups "stitching" the mud with their bills in search of food.

**Morphological Characteristics**
Tiny (12-14 cm), barely larger than a sparrow.
Short, straight black bill. Black legs.
In winter pale grey above and white below. In spring it takes on rusty/reddish tones on the breast and back.

**Behavior**
Very tame, allows close approach. Feeds quickly by pecking at the mud surface rather than probing deeply.

**Diet**
Microscopic invertebrates, dipteran larvae.

**Reproduction**
Nests in the Arctic tundra.

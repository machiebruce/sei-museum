The Mallard is the most well-known and widespread wild duck, the ancestor of most domestic ducks. Adaptable and resilient, it is present in almost every body of water.

#### **Distribution and Habitat**

**Global Distribution**
Cosmopolitan in the Northern Hemisphere.

**Preferred Habitat**
Any type of wetland: rivers, lakes, urban ponds, marine coasts, salt pans.

**Presence in Sicily**
Very common sedentary and breeding species.

**Status in the Salt Pans**
Ubiquitous. Frequents both basins and channels. Females are often seen with offspring in spring.

**Morphological Characteristics**
Sturdy dabbling duck (50-65 cm).
Strong sexual dimorphism.
Male: **iridescent emerald green head**, white collar, chestnut breast, grey body, black curled tail feathers. Yellow bill.
Female: mottled brown cryptic plumage, orange and black bill.
Both sexes have the "speculum" (wing patch) blue-violet bordered with white.

**Behavior**
Dabbling duck (does not dive, but submerges only head and neck by tipping up). The male emits a soft whistle, the female the classic "quack-quack".

**Diet**
Omnivorous: seeds, aquatic plants, insects, bread (in urban areas), small mollusks.

**Reproduction**
Nests on the ground near water, among dense vegetation. Broods are large (9-13 eggs).

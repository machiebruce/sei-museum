The Great Crested Grebe is famous for its spectacular courtship display ("mirror dance") in which the pair face each other shaking their heads and offering each other algae. It is the aristocrat of the waters.

#### **Distribution and Habitat**

**Global Distribution**
Palearctic, Africa, Australia.

**Preferred Habitat**
Large lakes, artificial reservoirs, calm coastal areas in winter.

**Presence in Sicily**
Regular winter visitor and localized breeder (e.g., Lake Pozzillo, Plain of Catania).

**Status in the Salt Pans**
Regular winter guest in the deeper basins or in the sea off the salt pans.

**Morphological Characteristics**
The largest of European grebes (46-51 cm). Long slender white neck.
In breeding plumage: unmistakable **black ear tufts and chestnut and black ruff** around the face.
In winter: white and grey, loses the ruff but keeps the brilliant white neck. Pointed pink bill.

**Behavior**
Excellent diver. Often carries chicks on its back ("back-riding") to protect and keep them warm in the first weeks of life.

**Diet**
Mainly fish, which it pursues underwater.

**Reproduction**
Nests on floating platforms anchored to reed beds.

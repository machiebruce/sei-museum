The Grey Heron is the most common and widespread of the European herons. With its unmistakable silhouette and grey-toned plumage, it is a constant presence in wetland areas, often seen motionless like a statue waiting for prey.

#### **Distribution and Habitat**

**Global Distribution**
Palearctic species with a vast range covering much of Europe, Asia and parts of Africa. Northern populations tend to migrate south in winter, while southern ones are mostly resident.

**Preferred Habitat**
Extremely adaptable, it frequents almost every type of wetland: rivers, lakes, ponds, rice paddies, brackish lagoons and marine coasts. It needs shallow water for fishing and tall trees or quiet reedbeds for nesting.

**Presence in Sicily**
In Sicily it is a common species both as a breeder and as a winterer and migrant. Coastal wetlands, such as the Saline di Trapani e Paceco, offer ideal refuge.

**Status in the Salt Pans**
At the Ettore Infersa Salt Pans it is a regular and easily observable presence all year round. It is often seen perched on the banks of the pools or motionless in the shallow water of the perimeter channels while hunting.

**Morphological Characteristics**
Large heron with a robust body. The plumage is predominantly grey above and whitish-grey below.
Length 90-98 cm, wingspan 175-195 cm, weight 1-2 kg.
The neck is long and white with black streaks; the head is white with a black stripe above the eye ending in a long nuchal crest. Yellow-orange beak (more intense in the breeding period) and brown legs.

**Sexual Dimorphism**
Substantially absent. Males and females are similar in plumage, with males tending to be slightly larger.

**Behavior**
Hunts mainly by ambush, remaining motionless for long periods or walking very slowly. The flight is powerful, with deep wingbeats and the neck retracted in an "S" shape, a characteristic that distinguishes it from storks (which fly with their necks extended).

**Feeding**
Opportunistic. It feeds mainly on fish, but the diet also includes amphibians, small mammals (voles), insects, reptiles and even small birds. It swallows prey whole, head first.

**Reproduction**
Often nests in colonies (heronries), sometimes mixed with other ardeids. It builds large stick nests on treetops or in reedbeds.
Lays 3-5 eggs between February and April. Incubation lasts about 25 days and chicks leave the nest after about 50 days.

<div class="species-description-grid">
  <div>
    <strong>Historical Threats</strong>
    <p>In the past it was persecuted because it was considered harmful to fish farming. The destruction of wetlands has historically reduced areas suitable for nesting.</p>
  </div>
  
  <div>
    <strong>Current Threats</strong>
    <p>Today the species is in a good state of conservation. The main threats remain anthropic disturbance near nesting colonies, illegal killing, and water contamination by pesticides and heavy metals.</p>
  </div>
</div>

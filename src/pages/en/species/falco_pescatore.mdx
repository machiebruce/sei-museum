The only representative of its family, the Osprey is an absolute specialist: a raptor evolved exclusively to catch fish, equipped with unique feet in the animal kingdom.

#### **Distribution and Habitat**

**Global Distribution**
Cosmopolitan (present on all continents except Antarctica).

**Preferred Habitat**
Sea coasts, lakes, large rivers with clear waters rich in fish.

**Presence in Sicily**
Mainly regular migrant and winter visitor. Recently returned to nest in some Mediterranean areas thanks to reintroduction projects.

**Status in the Salt Pans**
Observable especially during migrations while perching on poles fixed in the basins to consume its prey.

**Morphological Characteristics**
Medium-large raptor (55-58 cm, wingspan 145-170 cm).
White underparts, dark brown upperparts. White head with a prominent **black band through the eye** (mask).
Long angled wings, held in an "M" shape in flight. Bluish legs with hooked talons and reversible toes for gripping slippery fish.

**Behavior**
Hunts by spotting fish from above (hovering or gliding), then dives feet-first, fully submerging. When it emerges, it shakes water from its feathers and orients the fish "torpedo-style" (head forward) for aerodynamics.

**Diet**
Exclusively fish (mullet, sea bass).

**Reproduction**
Builds huge nests on isolated trees, power poles or cliffs.

<div class="species-description-grid">

    <strong>Historic Threats</strong>

  </div>
  
  <div>
    <strong>Current Threats</strong>
    <p>Disturbance at nests, impact with power lines, reduction of fish stocks.</p>

</div>

**Conservation Status**
IUCN: LC. Recovering in Europe.

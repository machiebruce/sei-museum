The Little Grebe is the smallest of the European grebes. As its name suggests, it is a skilled underwater swimmer that disappears beneath the surface at the slightest sign of danger, only to resurface dozens of meters away.

#### **Distribution and Habitat**

**Global Distribution**
Europe, Africa and Asia.

**Preferred Habitat**
Fresh or brackish water bodies with dense vegetation. In winter it also moves to more open and coastal waters.

**Presence in Sicily**
Resident and breeder.

**Status in the Salt Pans**
Present in the channels and basins. Its shrill call ("trill") is often heard before it is seen.

**Morphological Characteristics**
Very small (25-29 cm), floating "powder-puff" appearance.
In summer: intense chestnut-red cheeks and neck, yellow patch at the base of the bill.
In winter: dull coloration, light brown-beige.
No visible tail, the body ends bluntly.

**Behavior**
Shy. Dives frequently to feed or escape predators.

**Diet**
Aquatic insects, larvae, small fish and tadpoles caught by diving.

**Reproduction**
Builds a floating nest of rotting plant material that heats the eggs. Covers the eggs with algae when leaving the nest to hide them.

The Smew is a small, very elegant fishing duck ("sawbill"). Males, with their ice-white plumage marked in black, are called "nuns" in some regions for their striking appearance.

#### **Distribution and Habitat**

**Global Distribution**
Breeds in the taiga (Scandinavia, Russia). Winters in central and southern Europe.

**Preferred Habitat**
Lakes, slow rivers, sheltered coastal lagoons and salt pans in winter.

**Presence in Sicily**
Rare and irregular winter visitor. Its presence is often linked to cold spells in northern Europe.

**Status in the Salt Pans**
Occasional but valuable winter guest in the salt pan basins.

**Morphological Characteristics**
Small duck (38-44 cm).
Male: **pure white with a black mask over the eye** and fine black lines on the back and flanks. White nuchal crest.
Female: grey body, chestnut-colored head ("redhead") with white cheeks.
Short grey bill with **serrated edges** to hold fish.

**Behavior**
Excellent diver. Often gregarious in small groups.

**Diet**
Small fish, aquatic larvae.

**Reproduction**
Nests in tree cavities (often old Black Woodpecker nests) near water in boreal forests.

The Stone-curlew is a mysterious bird with crepuscular and nocturnal habits. With its cryptic plumage and large yellow eyes, it is perfectly adapted to arid and semi-desert environments.

#### **Distribution and Habitat**

**Global Distribution**
Southern Europe, North Africa, western Asia.

**Preferred Habitat**
Open arid, steppe areas, dry riverbeds, extensive cultivated fields and back-dune areas.

**Presence in Sicily**
Resident and partially migratory. Common in suitable inland and coastal areas.

**Status in the Salt Pans**
It frequents the uncultivated and dry areas at the edges of the salt pans and the Reserve (e.g. Pre-reserve), camouflaging among stones and dry grass.

**Morphological Characteristics**
Atypical medium-sized wader (40-44 cm).
Sand-colored plumage with black streaks, incredibly camouflaged on the ground.
Distinctive feature: **enormous yellow eyes** (adaptation for night vision) and yellow beak with black tip. Robust yellow legs with thick "knees."

**Behavior**
During the day it crouches motionless on the ground; it becomes active at sunset emitting a plaintive and powerful call reminiscent of the Curlew, audible at great distance. It runs quickly rather than flying.

**Diet**
Invertebrates (beetles, earthworms), small reptiles and frogs, hunted by sight at night.

**Reproduction**
Nests on the ground among stones; eggs and chicks are almost invisible.

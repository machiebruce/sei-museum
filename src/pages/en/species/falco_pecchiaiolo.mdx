The European Honey Buzzard is an exceptional migrant. Its name reveals its particular diet: it feeds mainly on wasp and bee larvae.

#### **Distribution and Habitat**

**Global Distribution**
Europe and Western Asia. Winters in tropical Africa.

**Preferred Habitat**
Deciduous or mixed woods with open clearings for hunting.

**Presence in Sicily**
Very common migrant, especially on the Strait of Messina (famous migratory bottleneck) and western Sicily during passages.

**Status in the Salt Pans**
Observed in transit in spring (May) and late summer (September). Often uses thermals above the Trapani hinterland, visible from the salt pans.

**Morphological Characteristics**
Similar to the Common Buzzard but slimmer (52-60 cm), with a small head ("pigeon-like") and long neck.
Extremely variable plumage (from almost white to dark brown).
Long tail with **three dark bands** (two at the base and one broad at the tip). Yellow eye (male) or brown (female/juvenile).

**Behavior**
Spends a lot of time on the ground digging up wasp nests. Its legs are protected by thick scales against stings and the feathers on its face are hard and compact.

**Diet**
Specialist: larvae and pupae of wasps, hornets and wild bees. Supplemented with reptiles and amphibians.

**Reproduction**
Nests on tall trees deep in the forest.

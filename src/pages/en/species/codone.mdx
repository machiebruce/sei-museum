Considered by many the most elegant duck for its slender silhouette and long thin neck. The name derives from the male's long pointed tail, similar to a pin.

#### **Distribution and Habitat**

**Global Distribution**
Northern Hemisphere (Holarctic).

**Preferred Habitat**
Large open wetlands, estuaries, salt pans, flooded grasslands.

**Presence in Sicily**
Regular winter visitor and migrant.

**Status in the Salt Pans**
Regular winter guest, often in company of wigeons and mallards. Its elongated silhouette makes it recognizable even from a distance.

**Morphological Characteristics**
Large slim duck (51-62 cm).
Male: chocolate-colored head, white neck with a white stripe extending up the sides of the head. Gray body, bronze-green speculum. Tail with two long central black feathers (the "pin").
Female: brown mottled, but with long neck and elegant gray beak.

**Behavior**
Fast and agile flight. It feeds by tipping up in the water (dabbling) to reach the bottom thanks to its long neck, without needing to fully submerge.

**Diet**
Aquatic plants, seeds, rhizomes, invertebrates.

**Reproduction**
Nests on the ground in open areas.

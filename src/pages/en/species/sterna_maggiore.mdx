The Caspian Tern is the largest tern in the world. Imposing and powerful, it is the size of a Herring Gull but with the elegance of a tern. Its enormous red bill is unmistakable.

#### **Distribution and Habitat**

**Global Distribution**
Cosmopolitan but patchy distribution (North America, Baltic, Black Sea, Africa, Australia).

**Preferred Habitat**
Marine coasts, large lakes, salt pans and fish-rich lagoons.

**Presence in Sicily**
Regular winter visitor and migrant.

**Status in the Salt Pans**
Regularly observed in winter. Often rests on islets in the middle of the basins, towering above gulls and other terns.

**Morphological Characteristics**
Very large (48-56 cm, wingspan up to 140 cm).
Sturdy white and pale grey body. Black cap (streaked with white in winter).
Distinctive feature: **enormous coral-red bill**, thick and pointed (sometimes with a black tip). Black legs.

**Behavior**
Powerful yet agile flight. Emits a hoarse, deep call ("kraah") similar to that of a heron. Fishes by diving violently into the water.

**Diet**
Fish, sometimes of considerable size.

**Reproduction**
Nests in colonies on isolated sandy or gravel islands.

The Eurasian Spoonbill looks from afar like a white heron, but its behavior and above all its incredible bill make it unique. It is not a true heron, but is related to the Ibises.

#### **Distribution and Habitat**

**Global Distribution**
From western Europe to eastern Asia and India.

**Preferred Habitat**
Freshwater or brackish wetlands with vast reed beds and shallow muddy bottoms.

**Presence in Sicily**
Mainly a regular migrant and winter visitor. Nesting is irregular but increasing.

**Status in the Salt Pans**
Regular guest, especially during migration and in winter. Often seen in groups sweeping the water in unison.

**Morphological Characteristics**
Large white bird (80-90 cm).
Unmistakable **black, long, flat bill, widened at the tip in a spoon shape** (yellow at the tip in adults).
In breeding plumage it has a yellowish nuchal crest and a yellow patch on the chest.

**Behavior**
Feeds by walking in water and moving its half-open bill from side to side ("scything") to feel prey by touch, as it often hunts in murky waters.

**Diet**
Small fish, crustaceans, aquatic beetles, larvae.

**Reproduction**
Nests in colonies in reed beds or on trees (willows, tamarisks). Bulky nests made of reeds and branches.

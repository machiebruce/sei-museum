The Yellow-legged Gull (often called "cocale" in Sicily) is the dominant large coastal gull in the Mediterranean. Strong, adaptable and often aggressive, it is the undisputed master of coastal skies.

#### **Distribution and Habitat**

**Global Distribution**
Mediterranean basin, Black Sea and adjacent Atlantic coasts.

**Preferred Habitat**
Marine coasts, islands, cliffs, but has adapted perfectly to coastal cities.

**Presence in Sicily**
Sedentary and very common breeder everywhere along the coast and on the islands around Sicily.

**Status in the Salt Pans**
Ubiquitous. Uses the embankments for nesting and resting. Often preys on eggs or chicks of other birds (waders, terns) in the salt pans.

**Morphological Characteristics**
Large and robust (55-67 cm, wingspan 130-158 cm).
Adults: silver-grey mantle, white head and body, black wingtips with white "mirrors". **Bright yellow legs** (distinction from the northern Herring Gull which has pink legs) and yellow bill with a red spot on the lower mandible.
Juveniles: brown mottled, taking 4 years to reach adult plumage.

**Behavior**
Dominant. Emits the classic call ("laughing call"). Very territorial.

**Diet**
Omnivorous and opportunistic: fish, carrion, refuse, birds, small mammals, olives.

**Reproduction**
Nests in colonies on cliffs or rooftops. Defends the nest with aggressive dives even against humans.

The Sandwich Tern is a larger tern, with a more "angry" profile due to its ruffled nuchal crest. Its scientific name *sandvicensis* recalls that it was first described at Sandwich in Kent.

#### **Distribution and Habitat**

**Global Distribution**
Atlantic, Mediterranean and Black Sea coasts.

**Preferred Habitat**
Exclusively coastal: brackish lagoons, estuaries and sandy beaches.

**Presence in Sicily**
Common winter visitor and migrant. Irregular but possible breeding.

**Status in the Salt Pans**
Very common in winter. It is distinguished by its harsh call ("krrric") audible from a distance.

**Morphological Characteristics**
Larger than the Common Tern (37-43 cm).
Very white plumage. Head with a black cap forming a shaggy hind crest.
Unmistakable distinctive feature: **long black beak with a yellow tip**.

**Behavior**
Flies higher than other terns before diving. Often gregarious, forms compact groups ("clubs") on the ground.

**Feeding**
Fish (sardines, anchovies, sand smelts).

**Reproduction**
Nests in very dense colonies, often in association with gulls that offer indirect protection.

The Dunlin is probably the most common wader in European wetlands during winter. Its name derives from its unmistakable summer breeding plumage.

#### **Distribution and Habitat**

**Global Distribution**
Circumpolar (breeds in the Arctic).

**Preferred Habitat**
Breeds in tundra or peat bogs. In winter and migration frequents coastal mudflats, estuaries and salt pans.

**Presence in Sicily**
Abundant wintering and migratory.

**Status in the Salt Pans**
Forms large flocks in winter that perform spectacular synchronized aerial maneuvers over the pools, flashing alternately white and grey.

**Morphological Characteristics**
Medium-sized wader (16-22 cm).
Black beak slightly **curved downward** at the tip.
Winter: grey-brown above, white below.
Summer (breeding plumage): rust-colored back and, uniquely, a large **black patch on the belly** ("black-belly").

**Behavior**
Highly gregarious. Feeds by probing the mud with its beak ("stitching") in a rapid, rhythmic manner.

**Diet**
Worms, mollusks, insects.

**Reproduction**
Nests on the ground hidden in tundra grass tussocks.

The Grey Plover is the "silver" version of the Golden Plover, tied almost exclusively to salty coastal environments (from which its English name derives).

#### **Distribution and Habitat**

**Global Distribution**
Breeds in the high Arctic (tundra). Cosmopolitan in winter along coasts.

**Preferred Habitat**
Tidal flats, brackish lagoons, estuaries and salt pans. Rarely inland.

**Presence in Sicily**
Regular wintering and migratory.

**Status in the Salt Pans**
Common presence in winter. Solitary or in small scattered groups in the pools, noticeable for its grey-silver plumage.

**Morphological Characteristics**
Larger and more robust than the Golden Plover (27-30 cm). Sturdier beak.
Upperparts **grey-silver** speckled with white and black (never golden tones).
In flight shows **black axillaries (underwing)**, an infallible diagnostic feature. White rump.
In summer it has a black belly like the Golden Plover.

**Behavior**
More solitary than the Golden Plover. Very alert and wary.

**Diet**
Marine worms (polychaetes), mollusks, crustaceans.

**Reproduction**
Nests in the Arctic tundra.

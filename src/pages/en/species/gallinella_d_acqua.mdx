Shyer than the Coot, the Common Moorhen likes to frequent the edges of reedbeds and riparian vegetation, moving with nervous jerky motions.

#### **Distribution and Habitat**

**Global Distribution**
Cosmopolitan (present on almost all continents).

**Preferred Habitat**
Small ponds, ditches, canals and wetlands with abundant emergent vegetation for hiding.

**Presence in Sicily**
Common sedentary and breeding species.

**Status in the Salt Pans**
Frequents mainly drainage canals and marginal areas with thick reedbeds, less visible in open basins.

**Morphological Characteristics**
Smaller than the Coot (30-35 cm).
Brownish-black plumage with an **irregular white line on the flanks**. White undertail (evident when it jerks its tail up).
Red bill with a yellow tip and red frontal shield. Green legs with a red "garter" on the tibia.

**Behavior**
Swims moving its head back and forth rhythmically. If frightened, it runs on the water to take refuge in the reeds.

**Diet**
Omnivorous: aquatic plants, berries, insects, spiders, small fish.

**Reproduction**
Nests among reeds or on low branches over water. Often has two or three broods per year; young from the first brood may help feed subsequent siblings.

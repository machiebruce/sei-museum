The Eurasian Teal is the smallest European duck. Known for its extremely fast and acrobatic flight, it is capable of taking off almost vertically from the water's surface if startled.

#### **Distribution and Habitat**

**Global Distribution**
Vast Palearctic range. It breeds in northern Europe and Asia, wintering in the south.

**Preferred Habitat**
Small freshwater or brackish water bodies with dense vegetation, marshes, canals and salt pans.

**Presence in Sicily**
Very common winter visitor and migrant.

**Status in the Salt Pans**
It is one of the most abundant ducks in winter. It is often seen in large flocks resting on banks or filtering mud in shallow pools.

**Morphological Characteristics**
Very small duck (34-38 cm).
Male: chestnut head with a broad **iridescent green band** from the eye down the nape, bordered with cream yellow. Finely vermiculated grey body, bright green speculum.
Female: mottled brown, but recognizable by its small size and green speculum.

**Behavior**
Gregarious. The male emits a liquid, crystal-clear whistle "crrit", very distinctive.

**Feeding**
Omnivorous: seeds of aquatic plants, small invertebrates.

**Reproduction**
Nests on the ground, well hidden in vegetation, generally in Northern Europe.

The lord of the reed beds. The Western Marsh Harrier is a large raptor that tirelessly patrols wetlands flying low over the vegetation in search of prey.

#### **Distribution and Habitat**

**Global Distribution**
Europe, Central Asia and Africa.

**Preferred Habitat**
Large wetlands with extensive reed beds (*Phragmites*), rice fields and lagoons.

**Presence in Sicily**
Resident (breeding) and migrant/winter visitor. The Trapani salt pans and Stagnone are crucial areas.

**Status in the Salt Pans**
It is the easiest raptor to observe in the area. Often flies over the perimeter channels or dry basins.

**Morphological Characteristics**
Large raptor (48-56 cm, wingspan 115-130 cm).
Typical silhouette with long broad wings held in a **"V" shape** during gliding flight.
Strong sexual dimorphism: male has tricolored wings (gray, brown, black) and gray tail; female is dark chocolate brown with cream/yellowish head and "epaulets".

**Behavior**
Hunts at low altitude, gliding and suddenly turning to surprise prey. During courtship, the pair performs the "food pass" in flight: the male drops the food and the female catches it mid-air by flipping over.

**Diet**
Small water birds (coot chicks, moorhen), small mammals, frogs.

**Reproduction**
Nests on the ground, hidden in dense reed beds.

The White Stork is a symbol of good luck and fertility in many cultures. A great migrant, it announces the arrival of spring and has a strong bond with humans, often nesting on roofs and bell towers.

#### **Distribution and Habitat**

**Global Distribution**
Europe, North Africa, Asia Minor and the Middle East. Winters in sub-Saharan Africa.

**Preferred Habitat**
Open agricultural areas, wet meadows, rice fields and edges of wetlands where it hunts while walking.

**Presence in Sicily**
Breeding (recovering thanks to conservation projects, e.g. Plain of Gela, Plain of Catania) and regular migrant.

**Status in the Salt Pans**
Regularly observed during migrations (spring and autumn) while resting on the banks or flying over the area using thermals.

**Morphological Characteristics**
Large bird (100-115 cm, wingspan up to 215 cm).
White plumage with black wing feathers. Long, straight and pointed bright red beak (black in juveniles). Long red legs.

**Behavior**
Flies with neck extended (unlike herons). It is mute, but communicates through "bill-clattering" (rapidly claps its beak producing a rhythmic sound like castanets) during courtship at the nest.

**Diet**
Generalist carnivore: grasshoppers, beetles, amphibians, reptiles, small mammals.

**Reproduction**
Nests on elevated structures (trees, power poles, buildings). The nest is a large platform of branches reused and expanded each year.

Similar to the Eurasian Curlew but more compact, the Whimbrel is a great traveler that undertakes very long migrations. Its beak, though long, is less extreme than that of its larger cousin.

#### **Distribution and Habitat**

**Global Distribution**
Breeds in the Arctic and subarctic tundra. Winters on the coasts of Africa, South America, Asia and Oceania.

**Preferred Habitat**
Rocky or sandy coasts, estuaries, salt pans, coastal grasslands.

**Presence in Sicily**
Regular migrant, occasionally wintering.

**Status in the Salt Pans**
It is mainly observed during passage (April-May), stopping to refuel in the pools or on the banks.

**Morphological Characteristics**
Smaller than the Eurasian Curlew (40-46 cm).
Long beak curved downward, but shorter than the larger species and bent only in the final part.
Key distinguishing feature: **striped head**, with a light central stripe on the crown and two dark lateral stripes (well-defined dark and light "eyebrows").

**Behavior**
Less wary than the Eurasian Curlew. It emits a rapid, sonorous trill "bi-bi-bi-bi-bi" in flight (reminiscent of a laugh).

**Feeding**
Crabs (which it shakes to break the shell), mollusks, insects and berries (during nesting in the tundra).

**Reproduction**
Nests on the ground in the tundra.

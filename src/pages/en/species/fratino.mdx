The Kentish Plover is a small wader symbolizing well-preserved beaches and coastal wetlands. Unfortunately, it is also one of the species most threatened by beach tourism and mechanical beach cleaning.

#### **Distribution and Habitat**

**Global Distribution**
Cosmopolitan, but with fragmented range. In Europe it is found along the Mediterranean, Atlantic and Black Sea coasts.

**Preferred Habitat**
Sandy beaches with dunes, dried mudflats, salt pans and coastal lagoons. Requires open spaces free of tall vegetation for nesting.

**Presence in Sicily**
Breeding and sedentary, with numerical increases during migrations.

**Status in the Salt Pans**
The Trapani and Paceco Salt Pans are one of the most important nesting sites in Italy for this species. It is observed running quickly along the embankments or on the dry mud of the basins.

**Morphological Characteristics**
Small wader (15-17 cm). Pale sandy-brown upperparts, white underparts. Male has a black forehead band and black lateral breast patches; female has browner patches. Black bill and dark legs.

**Behavior**
Runs rapidly in short bursts, then stops abruptly to peck at prey. Characteristic "broken-wing" display to distract predators from the nest.

**Diet**
Small invertebrates, insects, crustaceans and mollusks picked from the mud surface.

**Reproduction**
Nests in a simple scrape on open ground, often among pebbles or dry mud. The eggs are highly camouflaged.

**Conservation Status**
IUCN: LC (Least Concern), but declining in Europe.

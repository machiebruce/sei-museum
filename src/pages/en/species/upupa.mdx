Unmistakable for its fan-shaped crest and "butterfly" flight, the Eurasian Hoopoe is one of the most beloved birds. Its name derives from its characteristic "up-up-up" call.

#### **Distribution and Habitat**

**Global Distribution**
Europe, Asia, Africa.

**Preferred Habitat**
Open countryside with scattered trees, orchards, parks, olive groves. Avoids dense woods.

**Presence in Sicily**
Common summer breeder (March-September). Some individuals winter in the warmest areas.

**Status in the Salt Pans**
Frequents the edges of the salt pans, gardens and nearby cultivated areas, where it searches for food walking on the ground.

**Morphological Characteristics**
Size of a blackbird (26-28 cm).
Orange-pink plumage on head and chest. Wings and tail with broad white and black bands.
Characteristic erectile crest on the head with black tips. Long, thin, curved bill.

**Behavior**
Feeds on the ground. When alarmed or excited, it opens its crest into a fan. The flight is undulating and irregular, with slow wingbeats showing the black and white pattern.

**Diet**
Terrestrial insects (mole crickets, beetle larvae, ants) and lizards. Probes the ground with its bill.

**Reproduction**
Nests in cavities of trees, dry stone walls, rural buildings. Females and nestlings emit an unpleasant odor as a deterrent against predators.

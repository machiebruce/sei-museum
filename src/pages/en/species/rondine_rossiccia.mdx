The Red-rumped Swallow is a species of Mediterranean charm, similar to the Barn Swallow but with warmer colors and a more gliding flight. Its nest is an engineering work featuring an entrance tunnel.

#### **Distribution and Habitat**

**Global Distribution**
Southern Europe, Africa, Asia.

**Preferred Habitat**
Rocky hilly areas, bridges, isolated old rural buildings, coasts.

**Presence in Sicily**
Migrant and breeder (more localized than the Barn Swallow).

**Status in the Salt Pans**
Occasionally observed hunting alongside other swallows and martins.

**Morphological Characteristics**
Similar to the Barn Swallow but with a **rusty-orange rump** (not white like the House Martin, nor dark like the Barn Swallow).
Rusty nape, lightly streaked cream underparts. Black undertail.

**Behavior**
Slower, more gliding flight than the Barn Swallow.
Builds a mud nest shaped like a "flask" or half-amphora, with a long entrance tunnel, attached to rock or ceilings.

**Diet**
Aerial insects.

**Reproduction**
Nests solitarily or in small scattered colonies.

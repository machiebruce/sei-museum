The European Bee-eater is arguably the most colorful bird in Europe, with plumage that looks tropical. It is a summer migrant that announces the arrival of the warm season with its liquid, melodious calls while hunting insects in flight.

#### **Distribution and Habitat**

**Global Distribution**
Southern Europe, North Africa, western Asia. Winters in tropical Africa.

**Preferred Habitat**
Open countryside, agricultural areas with tree rows, sand quarries, riverbanks.

**Presence in Sicily**
Common summer migrant and breeder (April-August).

**Status in the Salt Pans**
Frequents the areas surrounding the salt pans, often perching on power lines or dead trees to hunt dragonflies and bees over the water surfaces.

**Morphological Characteristics**
Medium size (27-29 cm).
Kaleidoscopic plumage: **chestnut and yellow back, blue and green wings, yellow throat edged with black, turquoise belly**.
Black bill, long and slightly curved. Red eye. Green tail with two elongated central tail feathers.

**Behavior**
Gregarious, lives in colonies. Hunts insects in flight with spectacular acrobatics. Before eating stinging insects (bees, wasps), it repeatedly beats them against the perch to remove the venom.

**Diet**
Hymenoptera (bees, wasps, hornets), dragonflies, butterflies. The scientific name *apiaster* means "bee-eater".

**Reproduction**
Nests in colonies by digging deep tunnels (up to 1-2 meters) in vertical sandy walls or sloping ground.

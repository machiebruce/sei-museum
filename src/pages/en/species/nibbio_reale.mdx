The Red Kite is unmistakable for its elegance and deeply forked tail. It is a predominantly European species, considered almost endemic to our continent.

#### **Distribution and Habitat**

**Global Distribution**
Almost exclusively European (target species for European conservation).

**Preferred Habitat**
Undulating open countryside with scattered copses, pastures.

**Presence in Sicily**
Breeding and resident (with significant populations in inland hilly areas).

**Status in the Salt Pans**
Less frequent than the Black Kite, but observable while it patrols the open countryside in the hinterland of the salt pans.

**Morphological Characteristics**
Larger and more colorful than the Black Kite (60-65 cm).
Light grey head, rusty-reddish streaked body, large white patches under the wings ("windows") visible in flight.
**Reddish and deeply forked** "V" tail.

**Behavior**
Tireless glider. The tail acts as a rudder, continuously rotating to correct course.

**Diet**
Mainly carrion and dead animals, small mammals, injured birds.

**Reproduction**
Nests on large trees (oaks, beeches).

**Conservation Status**
IUCN: LC (but population declining in some areas).

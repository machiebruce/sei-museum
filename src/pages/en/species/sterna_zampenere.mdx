The Gull-billed Tern is unusual because, despite being a tern, its feeding habits are less tied to water than those of its "cousins". It is often seen hunting insects or lizards on dry land.

#### **Distribution and Habitat**

**Global Distribution**
Cosmopolitan but fragmented.

**Preferred Habitat**
Salt pans, lagoons, but also cultivated fields, salt steppes and inland areas.

**Presence in Sicily**
Migrant and breeder (e.g., wetlands of southeastern Sicily).

**Status in the Salt Pans**
Present during migrations and the summer period.

**Morphological Characteristics**
Robust and compact tern (35-38 cm).
White and pale grey plumage. In breeding plumage it has a black cap.
Distinctive feature: **black, short, very sturdy bill** (similar to that of a gull, hence the name *Gull-billed*), and black legs.

**Behavior**
Powerful flight. Often hunts by swooping onto the ground or skimming the water, but rarely dives.

**Diet**
Opportunistic: large insects (grasshoppers, dragonflies), lizards, frogs, chicks of other birds, small mammals.

**Reproduction**
Nests in colonies on islets or banks.

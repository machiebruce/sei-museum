The Slender-billed Gull is a medium-sized gull, elegant and very pale in appearance, characterized by a long, slender bill and exceptionally long red legs.  
It belongs to the order *Charadriiformes* and the family *Laridae*.  
It is a typical species of the Mediterranean and coastal wetlands.

#### **Appearance and Physical Characteristics**

- **Size:** Medium, but very slender in appearance. Length 39-47 cm, wingspan 100-110 cm.
- **Plumage:**
- **Adult:** The general appearance is **pale and contrasting**. The **underparts (belly, chest, head) are pure white** which may take on, in spring, a **delicate rosy hue** (hence the name). The **upperparts (back, wings) are a very pale, almost pearl grey**, lighter than any other European gull.
- **Bill:** **Very long, slender and straight**, **dark red or blackish** in adults. It almost looks like forceps.
- **Legs:** **Exceptionally long, slender and bright red** (redder than those of the Black-headed Gull). They are clearly visible in flight and give it an elegant carriage.
Its diet is specialized and reflects the shape of the bill:  
- **Small aquatic animals:** **Crustaceans** (brine shrimp, shrimps), **aquatic insects**, **small fish** and **marine worms**. It is less opportunistic and less dependent on refuse than other gulls.

#### **Distribution and Habitat**

- **In Italy:** **Rare and localized breeder, irregular migrant and winter visitor**. The main colonies are in **Sardinia** (Stagni di Molentargius, Sale 'e Porcus), **Apulia** (Saline di Margherita di Savoia) and **Sicily** (Trapani Salt Pans). After breeding, it disperses and can be observed irregularly in other coastal wetlands.
- **Habitat:** During breeding it is strictly tied to **lagoons, salt pans, large coastal ponds with sandy or muddy islets completely devoid of vegetation**. Outside the season, it also frequents beaches, estuaries and, rarely, landfills.

#### **Diet**

- **Feeding technique:** Often feeds **walking in shallow water** (thanks to its long legs), probing the mud or water with its long bill, or chasing small prey. It rarely dives.

#### **Behavior and Reproduction**

- **Nesting:** Its distribution is fragmented. It breeds in colonies around the **Mediterranean Sea**, the **Black Sea**, the **Caspian Sea** and in parts of central and southern Asia.
- **Migration:** It is a **partial migrant**. The more northern populations migrate south, while Mediterranean ones are often **sedentary or move little**.
- **Gregariousness:** It is a **strongly gregarious** species. It nests in **dense colonies** on sandy islets, often together with **Mediterranean Gulls and Common Terns**.
- **Nesting:** The nest is a simple depression in the sand, sometimes with a bit of vegetation. The colony is vulnerable to disturbance and tides.
- **Flight:** Light and elegant flight. In flight, the contrast between the white body, very pale grey wings, long red legs and dark bill is very characteristic.
- **Voice:** Its call is a nasal, sharp **"kee-ah"** or **"kwee-ehr"**, less shrill than that of the Black-headed Gull.
**Anthropogenic disturbance** in nesting colonies: it is **extremely sensitive**. Human presence (tourists, bathers, photographers) on sandy islets can cause the reproductive failure of entire colonies.  
**Predation** of eggs and chicks by crows, Yellow-legged Gulls, rats and feral dogs.  
**Alteration of water levels** in salt pans (for salt production) which can flood or isolate nests.  
**Habitat loss** (abandonment of salt pan management, coastal erosion).  
**Water pollution**.

#### **Status and Conservation**

- **IUCN Status:** Classified as **"Least Concern"** globally, but the European population is **small, localized and considered vulnerable**.
- **Threats (critical for colonies):**
In summary, the **Slender-billed Gull** is an elegant, specialized gull with an almost "wader-like" carriage. Its survival in Italy depends on the absolute protection of a few precious colonies on remote islets of our salt pans, making it one of the rarest and most sought-after gull species by birdwatchers.

#### **Curiosities and Summary**

**Crucial distinguishing feature:** **It completely lacks the dark summer hood** typical of other gulls (Mediterranean, Black-headed). The head is always white, with only a **thin black mask around the eye**, giving it a "sulky appearance".  
**Wings:** In flight, the pale grey wings have **black tips**, but **without white spots** on the primaries.  
**Juveniles:** Similar to adults but with a marked dark "W" pattern on the wings (dark coverts) and a black band on the tail.

**Where to see it in Italy**  
**Rare and localized breeder** only in a few colonies in Sardinia, Apulia and Sicily. **Irregularly sightable** as a migrant or winter visitor in other coastal wetlands (e.g. Po Delta, Venetian lagoons).  
**How to recognize it**  
1. **Pale general appearance** (very pale grey and white).  
2. **Head always white** (no dark hood).  
3. **Very long, slender, dark red bill**.  
4. **Very long red legs**.  
5. In flight, pale grey wings with **black tips without white spots**.  
**Slender-billed Gull:** **White head**, **long, slender** dark red bill, **long red legs**, very **pale** general coloration.  
**Black-headed Gull:** **Chocolate brown head**, **short** dark red bill, **short red legs**, darker general coloration.  
**Slender-billed Gull:** **White head**, **long, slender bill**.  
**Mediterranean Gull:** **Glossy black head**, **short, thick** crimson bill.

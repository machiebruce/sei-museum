The Northern Shoveler takes its name from its enormous spoon-shaped beak, a highly specialized tool for filtering plankton. It is an unmistakable duck both for its shape and the bright colors of the male.

#### **Distribution and Habitat**

**Global Distribution**
Holarctic (Europe, Asia, North America).

**Preferred Habitat**
Shallow fresh or brackish waters, rich in nutrients and vegetation, ideal for its feeding technique.

**Presence in Sicily**
Common wintering and migratory. Occasional breeding.

**Status in the Salt Pans**
Very present in the evaporation pools of the salt pans, where the water is rich in small invertebrates. They often swim in circles to stir up the bottom.

**Morphological Characteristics**
Medium-sized duck (44-52 cm).
Unique feature: **enormous black spatulate beak**.
Male: dark green head (similar to Mallard), pure white chest, chestnut-reddish flanks, yellow eye.
Female: brown mottled, but recognizable by the large beak.

**Behavior**
Swims with its beak immersed in water, filtering continuously. They often form groups that swim in circles ("whirlpool") to bring food to the surface using the generated current.

**Diet**
Plankton, small crustaceans, insect larvae, seeds. The beak has internal comb-like lamellae to retain food.

**Reproduction**
Nests in wet meadows near water.

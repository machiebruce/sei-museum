The Little Egret is a miniature version of the Great White Egret, but with a much livelier character. It is the most common heron to observe actively hunting in shallow water, often chasing prey by running.

#### **Distribution and Habitat**

**Global Distribution**
Wide distribution in southern Europe, Africa, Asia and Australia. European populations are partially migratory, wintering in the Mediterranean basin or Africa.

**Preferred Habitat**
Frequents a wide range of wetlands: rice fields, ditches, lagoons, salt pans and marine coasts.

**Presence in Sicily**
Very common throughout the island, both as a breeder and as a winter visitor.

**Status in the Salt Pans**
Ubiquitous in the Trapani and Marsala Salt Pans. Often observed in scattered groups along channels and in shallower basins.

**Morphological Characteristics**
Medium-sized heron (length 55-65 cm).
Entirely pure white plumage. Distinctive features are the black bill (slender and pointed) and black legs with **yellow feet** (visible when walking or flying).
In breeding plumage it develops two long filamentous plumes on the nape ("garzette").

**Behavior**
Unlike the Grey Heron, the Little Egret is very dynamic: it runs, shakes its feet in the water to scare fish and makes small leaps. It is gregarious, especially at evening roosts.

**Diet**
Small fish, amphibians, crustaceans and aquatic insects.

**Reproduction**
Nests in colonies (heronries) in trees or shrubs, often together with other ardeids. Lays 3-5 pale blue-green eggs.

The Black Kite is one of the most widespread raptors in the world. Opportunistic and adaptable, it is an excellent natural scavenger that often frequents wetlands looking for dead fish or refuse.

#### **Distribution and Habitat**

**Global Distribution**
Old World (Europe, Africa, Asia, Australia).

**Preferred Habitat**
Varied: wetlands, lake and river shores, landfills, agricultural areas.

**Presence in Sicily**
Regular migrant and breeding (declining population).

**Status in the Salt Pans**
Regular visitor. It flies over the pools gliding and maneuvering nimbly with its tail to look for floating dead fish.

**Morphological Characteristics**
Medium-sized raptor (55-60 cm).
Uniformly dark brown plumage.
Slightly **forked tail** (less than the Red Kite), which appears almost straight when spread open like a fan.

**Behavior**
Agile and maneuverable flight. Gregarious, especially during migration or in roosts. Often steals food from other birds (kleptoparasitism).

**Diet**
Omnivorous: fish (often dead), carrion, refuse, small mammals, birds, insects.

**Reproduction**
Nests in trees, often decorating the nest with rags, plastic or paper ("trash").

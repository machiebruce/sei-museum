The European Roller is a bird with exotic plumage, electric turquoise and cobalt blue. Despite its name, it is not related to jays (corvids), but is closer to kingfishers and bee-eaters.

#### **Distribution and Habitat**

**Global Distribution**
Southern and eastern Europe, western Asia, North Africa.

**Preferred Habitat**
Open countryside, steppe areas with scattered trees, wooded pastures.

**Presence in Sicily**
Summer migrant and breeder (typical of warm inland areas, but also present near the coast).

**Status in the Salt Pans**
Summer visitor in the agricultural areas surrounding the salt pans. Uses power lines as hunting perches.

**Morphological Characteristics**
Jackdaw-sized (30-32 cm).
Unmistakable: head and body **turquoise blue**, back light chestnut. Dark blue and bright turquoise wings (very visible in flight).
Robust black bill.

**Behavior**
Hunts from elevated perches, swooping down onto prey. The English name "Roller" derives from the aerial acrobatics (rolling) performed during courtship. Harsh voice similar to a corvid.

**Diet**
Large insects (grasshoppers, beetles), lizards, small snakes, scorpions.

**Reproduction**
Nests in tree cavities (often old oaks or almond trees), holes in walls or artificial nest boxes.

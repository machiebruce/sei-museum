The Glossy Ibis is the only Ibis regularly present in Europe. From afar it appears black, but up close, under the right light, its plumage explodes in metallic red, green and violet reflections.

#### **Distribution and Habitat**

**Global Distribution**
Cosmopolitan (present on all continents, very widespread in Australia).

**Preferred Habitat**
Freshwater marshes, rice paddies, flooded areas with low vegetation.

**Presence in Sicily**
Regular migrant and wintering. Localized nesting expanding (e.g. Lentini).

**Status in the Salt Pans**
Often observed in groups (even numerous) probing the soft mud of the pools or nearby marshes.

**Morphological Characteristics**
Medium size (55-65 cm).
Dark chestnut-reddish body with **metallic reflections** on the wings.
Long, dark beak **curved downward** (sickle-shaped). Long dark legs.

**Behavior**
Gregarious. Flies in "V" formations or undulating lines, alternating wing beats with short glides.

**Diet**
Aquatic invertebrates, larvae, leeches (from which it gets its name "mignattaio", leech-eater), amphibians.

**Reproduction**
Nests in mixed colonies with herons in reed beds or in trees.

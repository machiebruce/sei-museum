The Pied Avocet is the emblem of elegance in the salt pans, unmistakable for its black and white plumage and singular upturned beak. It is a symbol of wetland protection (logo of the RSPB reserve in the UK).

#### **Distribution and Habitat**

**Global Distribution**
Palearctic species. Breeds in temperate zones of Europe and central Asia.

**Preferred Habitat**
Closely linked to shallow brackish or salty waters and coastal lagoons with mudflats. Salt pans are its habitat of choice.

**Presence in Sicily**
Localized breeder and regular winter visitor. The Trapani Salt Pans host one of the most important populations in Italy.

**Status in the Salt Pans**
Iconic species of the area. Visible sweeping the water with its beak in the evaporation pools.

**Morphological Characteristics**
Medium-large wader (42-45 cm).
Highly graphic black and white contrasting plumage. Long grey-blue legs.
The unique feature is the **black, thin, upward-curved beak**.

**Behavior**
Gregarious and noisy during nesting. It aggressively defends its chicks. It swims well thanks to partially webbed feet (rare among waders).

**Feeding**
Uses its beak like a scythe, moving it laterally at water level to filter small crustaceans, worms and larvae from liquid mud.

**Reproduction**
Nests in colonies on mud islets or bare banks. Lays 3-4 eggs on the ground in a simple scrape.

**Conservation Status**
IUCN: LC (Least Concern). In Italy it is included in Annex I of the EU Birds Directive.

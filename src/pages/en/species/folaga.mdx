The Eurasian Coot is a familiar presence in almost every body of water across Europe. Often confused with ducks, it is actually a rail, more closely related to moorhens and cranes. It is known for its aggressive and territorial behavior.

#### **Distribution and Habitat**

**Global Distribution**
Vast Eurasian, North African and Australian range.

**Preferred Habitat**
Still or slow-flowing waters: lakes, ponds, canals, marshes and artificial reservoirs.

**Presence in Sicily**
Very common sedentary and breeding species. In winter, local populations are joined by migratory contingents from the north.

**Status in the Salt Pans**
Very abundant. Often observed swimming in large groups ("rafts") in the deeper basins or grazing on the embankments.

**Morphological Characteristics**
Rounded waterbird (36-39 cm), entirely sooty black plumage.
Distinctive feature: **white bill extending into a white frontal shield** on the forehead. Ruby-red eye.
Robust greenish legs with long lobed toes (not webbed) allowing it to swim and walk on mud or floating vegetation.

**Behavior**
To take off it must clumsily run across the water surface beating its wings ("pattering"). It dives with a little leap to search for food on the bottom.

**Diet**
Omnivorous but predominantly vegetarian (aquatic plants, algae), supplemented with mollusks and insects.

**Reproduction**
Builds floating nests anchored to vegetation. Very prolific, chicks have a bizarre red and orange head.

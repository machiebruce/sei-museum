The Black-headed Gull is the small gull that throngs coastlines, ports and cities in winter. The name "common" should not mislead: in summer it changes appearance, donning a dark brown hood.

#### **Distribution and Habitat**

**Global Distribution**
Vast Palearctic range.

**Preferred Habitat**
Coasts, lakes, rivers, wetlands and urban areas. Less pelagic (open-sea) than the larger gulls.

**Presence in Sicily**
Very abundant winter visitor. Rare/irregular breeder.

**Status in the Salt Pans**
Massive presence in winter. Hundreds of individuals gather on the embankments or swim in the basins.

**Morphological Characteristics**
Small gull (35-39 cm).
Winter: white/grey body, white head with a **dark spot behind the eye** (ear patch). Dark red bill and legs.
Summer (breeding plumage): dark chocolate brown hood (not black) covering the face but not the nape.
In flight it shows a white wedge on the leading edge of the wing.

**Behavior**
Noisy and gregarious. Opportunistic.

**Diet**
Omnivorous: fish, invertebrates, refuse, insects. Often follows tractors in fields to eat exposed worms.

**Reproduction**
Nests in colonies in wetlands (generally in northern/central Europe).

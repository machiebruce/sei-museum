Often confused with the Kentish Plover or the Common Ringed Plover, the Little Ringed Plover is a regular visitor of inland waters and river gravel beds, recognizable by the yellow ring around the eye.

#### **Distribution and Habitat**

**Global Distribution**
Palearctic (Europe and Asia) and North Africa.

**Preferred Habitat**
Prefers freshwater: banks of gravelly rivers, flooded quarries, artificial basins, but also frequents salt pans during migration.

**Presence in Sicily**
Breeding and migrant.

**Status in the Salt Pans**
Less tied to brackish environments compared to the Kentish Plover, but present along channels and areas with freshwater input.

**Morphological Characteristics**
Similar to the Common Ringed Plover but slimmer (14-17 cm).
Distinctive feature: prominent yellow eye-ring (absent in the Common Ringed Plover).
Has a complete black collar around the neck and a black band on the forehead separated from the brown cap by a white line. Black beak (without orange base) and flesh-colored/pinkish legs (not orange).

**Behavior**
Solitary or in pairs, rarely in large flocks. Restless and active.

**Diet**
Insects, spiders, small worms.

**Reproduction**
Nests on gravel or sand near freshwater.

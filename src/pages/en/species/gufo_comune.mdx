The Long-eared Owl is the classic owl of collective imagination, with long "horns" on its head and orange eyes. It is strictly nocturnal and highly camouflaged during the day.

#### **Distribution and Habitat**

**Global Distribution**
Northern hemisphere.

**Preferred Habitat**
Coniferous or mixed woodlands near open areas for hunting, urban parks, tree-lined avenues.

**Presence in Sicily**
Resident and wintering.

**Status in the Salt Pans**
It shelters during the day in copses of pines or eucalyptuses at the edges of the salt pans. In winter it often forms "roosts" where dozens of individuals sleep on the same tree.

**Morphological Characteristics**
Medium size (35-40 cm).
Brown mottled plumage (perfect camouflage with tree bark).
Long **erect ear tufts** (which it can lower when relaxed). **Intense orange eyes**. Well-marked facial disc.

**Behavior**
During the day it freezes and stretches upright ("making the post") to look like a broken branch. Silent flight.

**Diet**
Small rodents (mice, voles).

**Reproduction**
Uses old crow or magpie nests in trees. Does not build its own nest.

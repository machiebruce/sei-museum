The Common Shelduck is a surface-feeding duck with an unmistakable, almost "drawn" appearance, intermediate in size between a duck and a goose. Its name probably derives from the fact that it often nests in abandoned fox burrows.

#### **Distribution and Habitat**

**Global Distribution**
Coasts of northwestern Europe and inland areas of central Asia.

**Preferred Habitat**
Sandy or muddy marine coasts, estuaries, salt pans and brackish lagoons.

**Presence in Sicily**
Common winter visitor and increasing breeder in coastal wetlands.

**Status in the Salt Pans**
One of the most characteristic ducks of the Trapani Salt Pans, present year-round. Pairs are often seen leading their offspring in the basins.

**Morphological Characteristics**
Large duck (60 cm).
Dark green head (appearing black), bright red bill, chestnut band across the chest contrasting with the white body. Black longitudinal band on the belly.
The male in spring has a prominent fleshy knob at the base of the bill.

**Behavior**
Territorial during nesting. Ducklings often gather in "nurseries" (crèches) supervised by a few adults while the other parents go to feed.

**Diet**
Feeds by filtering mud in search of small mollusks (particularly *Hydrobia*), crustaceans and algae.

**Reproduction**
Unusual habit of nesting in cavities: rabbit or fox burrows, tree holes or under dense bushes, sometimes even under buildings or structures in the salt pans.

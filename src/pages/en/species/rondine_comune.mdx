"One swallow does not make a summer", yet their arrival is undeniably tied to nature's rebirth. An aerial acrobat par excellence, the Barn Swallow is closely linked to rural human settlements.

#### **Distribution and Habitat**

**Global Distribution**
Cosmopolitan. Breeds in the northern hemisphere and winters in the southern hemisphere.

**Preferred Habitat**
Open countryside, farms, barns. Hunts over meadows and water bodies.

**Presence in Sicily**
Very common summer migrant and breeder.

**Status in the Salt Pans**
Hunts insects (midges, mosquitoes) skimming the water surface above the salt pan basins, often drinking in flight.

**Morphological Characteristics**
Small and slender (17-19 cm).
Dark metallic blue upperparts, forehead and **throat brick-red**, separated from the cream-white belly by a dark blue band across the chest.
Deeply forked tail with two long outer streamers (longer in males).

**Behavior**
Flies fast and low to the ground. Builds open-cup mud nests inside buildings (barns, porches, warehouses).

**Diet**
Exclusively insectivorous (hunts in flight).

**Reproduction**
Faithful to its nesting site. Lays 4-5 eggs.

<div class="species-description-grid">

**Conservation Status**
IUCN: LC (but declining).

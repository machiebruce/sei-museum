The Mediterranean Gull is one of the most beautiful and elegant species among Mediterranean larids. Often confused with the Black-headed Gull, it is distinguished by its intense black hood (not brown) and bright red bill.

#### **Distribution and Habitat**

**Global Distribution**
Almost endemic to the Western Palearctic, with strongholds in the Black Sea and Mediterranean.

**Preferred Habitat**
Marine coasts, lagoons, but also frequents inland cultivated fields.

**Presence in Sicily**
Winter visitor and migrant, with some breeding colonies.

**Status in the Salt Pans**
Frequent, especially in winter and spring.

**Morphological Characteristics**
Medium size (36-38 cm).
Adults in summer: **jet-black hood** extending down the nape, prominent white eyelids, robust red bill with a black band, white-grey wings without black tips (unlike the Black-headed Gull).
Adults in winter: white head with a dark patch behind the eye ("ear cap").

**Behavior**
Distinctive voice reminiscent of a nasal meow ("ee-u"). Less of a "scavenger" than other gulls.

**Diet**
Marine invertebrates, fish, but also insects and worms in ploughed fields.

**Reproduction**
Nests in dense colonies among low vegetation in lagoons.

The Common Ringed Plover is the "big brother" of the Little Ringed Plover and the Kentish Plover. Robust and active, it is a typical inhabitant of Northern European beaches that visits the Mediterranean in winter.

#### **Distribution and Habitat**

**Global Distribution**
Breeds in Northern Europe, Greenland and Canada. Winters in Southern Europe and Africa.

**Preferred Habitat**
Sandy or pebbly beaches, muddy tidal flats, salt pans.

**Presence in Sicily**
Common winter visitor and migrant.

**Status in the Salt Pans**
Very frequent in winter. It stands out for its bright orange legs as it runs on the dark mud.

**Morphological Characteristics**
Compact wader (18-20 cm).
Similar to the Little Ringed Plover but more robust.
Distinctive features: orange beak with black tip and orange legs (in the Little Ringed Plover they are pinkish/flesh-colored).
Full black collar on chest and well-defined black face mask. In flight shows a prominent white wing bar.

**Behavior**
Runs quickly in spurts. Territorial even in winter feeding areas.

**Diet**
Marine invertebrates, crustaceans, annelids. Uses sight to hunt and often "vibrates" its foot on the mud to make prey move.

**Reproduction**
Nests on the ground on pebble or sandy beaches.

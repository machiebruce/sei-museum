The Zitting Cisticola is a tiny bird difficult to see but very easy to hear: its jerky flight above the rushes is accompanied by a rhythmic "zip... zip... zip..." that sounds like scissors cutting through the air.

#### **Distribution and Habitat**

**Global Distribution**
Wide distribution in Africa, southern Europe, Asia and Australia.

**Preferred Habitat**
Wetlands with tall herbaceous vegetation, wet meadows, rush beds (Juncus), canal edges, cereal fields.

**Presence in Sicily**
Very common resident.

**Status in the Salt Pans**
Abundant among the halophytic vegetation (Salicornia, rushes) along the edges of pools and canals.

**Morphological Characteristics**
Very small (10 cm).
Brown plumage streaked with black and tawny. Short fan-shaped tail with white tips, often wagged.
Thin and curved beak.

**Behavior**
The male performs a characteristic "roller coaster" nuptial flight, emitting a "zitt" sound at each peak of the undulation.

**Feeding**
Insectivorous (small insects, spiders).

**Reproduction**
It builds a "pouch" or flask-shaped nest, skillfully weaving living leaves of tall grasses and using spider webs as binding.

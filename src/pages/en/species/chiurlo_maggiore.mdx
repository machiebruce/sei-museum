The Eurasian Curlew is the largest European wader, famous for its very long curved beak and its melancholic song echoing across heaths and wetlands.

#### **Distribution and Habitat**

**Global Distribution**
Europe and Asia (temperate and boreal zone).

**Preferred Habitat**
In winter it frequents large estuaries, tidal flats, salt pans and coastal grasslands. It nests in heaths and wet grasslands.

**Presence in Sicily**
Regular winter visitor and migrant.

**Status in the Salt Pans**
Majestic presence in the pools. Its bill allows it to reach prey hidden deep in the mud, inaccessible to other birds.

**Morphological Characteristics**
Remarkable size (50-60 cm, wingspan up to 100 cm).
Brown plumage, delicately mottled grey-beige, very cryptic.
Distinctive feature: **very long beak (up to 15 cm) and curved downward**. White rump visible in flight (forming a white wedge on the back).

**Behavior**
Very wary and cautious. It emits a sonorous call, a whistle "cur-liii" that gives it its English name (*Curlew*).

**Feeding**
Marine worms (polychaetes), crabs (which it dismembers with its beak), bivalve mollusks.

**Reproduction**
Nests on the ground in central-northern Europe.

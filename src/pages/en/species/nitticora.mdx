The Black-crowned Night Heron, whose name means "Night crow," is a stocky, compact heron with predominantly crepuscular and nocturnal habits. During the day it often remains hidden among the tree foliage.

#### **Distribution and Habitat**

**Global Distribution**
Cosmopolitan (present in almost all the world).

**Preferred Habitat**
Wooded banks of rivers, lakes and marshes with tree vegetation (willows, poplars) or dense reed beds for daytime rest.

**Presence in Sicily**
Breeding and migratory.

**Status in the Salt Pans**
It frequents the canals and areas bordering the salt pans where it finds trees or shrubs for roosting. It becomes active at sunset, when it can be heard calling in flight.

**Morphological Characteristics**
Medium-sized heron (58-65 cm), stocky appearance, short thick neck.
Adults: black back and crown, grey wings, white underparts. Very prominent **ruby-red eye**. In breeding season it has two long white nape plumes.
Juveniles: brown with dense white speckling (very camouflaged).

**Behavior**
Spends the day motionless in roosts in trees. At dusk it flies toward feeding areas emitting a hoarse "quock."

**Diet**
Opportunistic: fish, amphibians, insects, but also eggs and chicks of other birds.

**Reproduction**
Nests in colonies in trees or shrubs. Builds nests of branches.

The Purple Heron is the shy "cousin" of the Grey Heron. Less visible, it prefers to stay hidden in the thick of reedbeds, blending in perfectly thanks to its plumage.

#### **Distribution and Habitat**

**Global Distribution**
Southern and central Europe, Africa and southern Asia. In Europe it is a summer visitor (April-September), wintering in sub-Saharan Africa.

**Preferred Habitat**
Extensive reedbeds (Phragmites) in freshwater or brackish marshes.

**Presence in Sicily**
Regular migrant and localized breeder where vast reedbeds exist.

**Status in the Salt Pans**
Less frequent in open pools compared to other herons; it is more easily observed in the perimeter channels rich in marsh vegetation or at stream mouths.

**Morphological Characteristics**
Slightly smaller and slimmer than the Grey Heron (78-90 cm).
Very long and thin neck, narrow head.
Dark reddish-brown plumage with neck streaked with black. In flight the feet protrude well beyond the tail and the neck forms a sharply angled "S".

**Behavior**
Very elusive. When alarmed, it adopts the "retreat position", stretching its neck upward to blend in with dry reeds. Crepuscular.

**Feeding**
Fish, amphibians and insects. It hunts with extreme patience, remaining motionless for long periods.

**Reproduction**
It nests in colonies, often monospecific, in the heart of inaccessible reedbeds. The nest is a platform of broken reeds at low height over the water.

**Conservation Status**
IUCN: LC. In Europe the population is stable but dependent on the protection of reedbeds.

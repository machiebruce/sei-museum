The fastest animal in the world. The Peregrine Falcon is a perfect hunting machine, capable of reaching speeds exceeding 300 km/h in a dive. In the salt pans it finds an ideal abundance of prey.

#### **Distribution and Habitat**

**Global Distribution**
Cosmopolitan. One of the most widespread species in the world.

**Preferred Habitat**
Very varied: from coastal cliffs to mountains, to cities (skyscrapers). In the salt pans it uses towers or poles as perches.

**Presence in Sicily**
Resident and widespread. Nests on coastal cliffs and on islands (e.g. Favignana, Levanzo).

**Status in the Salt Pans**
Frequent visitor for hunting. The concentrations of waders and ducks in the basins attract this specialized predator.

**Morphological Characteristics**
Robust compact falcon (38-50 cm).
Slate gray back, white/cream underparts densely barred with black.
Head characterized by the black "helmet" with a broad **black mustache** on the white cheek.
Large dark eye.

**Behavior**
Hunts almost exclusively birds in flight. Spots prey from above, folds its wings and drops in a dizzying dive (*stoop*), striking the victim with clenched talons to stun or kill it instantly.

**Diet**
Medium-sized birds: pigeons, ducks, waders, gulls.

**Reproduction**
Does not build a nest; lays eggs on rocky ledges or niches in tall buildings.

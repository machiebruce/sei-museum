Often confused with the Barn Swallow, the Common House Martin is more "urban", nesting in large numbers under the eaves of buildings. It is easily distinguished by its pure white rump.

#### **Distribution and Habitat**

**Global Distribution**
Palearctic.

**Preferred Habitat**
Inhabited areas (even large cities), rocky areas, bridges.

**Presence in Sicily**
Migrant and common summer breeder.

**Status in the Salt Pans**
Frequent aerial hunter above the pools, often in mixed groups with swallows. It nests on nearby buildings (e.g. Nubia, Paceco).

**Morphological Characteristics**
More compact than the Barn Swallow (13 cm).
Upper parts bluish-black, underparts entirely white.
Distinctive feature: **rump (area above tail) pure white**, well visible in flight. Forked tail but short, without long tail feathers.

**Behavior**
Gregarious. It builds closed mud nests with only a small entrance hole, glued under ledges or balconies.

**Feeding**
Insectivore (flies, winged aphids caught at altitude).

**Reproduction**
Nests in dense colonies.

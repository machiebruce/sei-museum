The Great Cormorant is a formidable aquatic predator. It is often seen in the classic "wings spread" pose on poles or rocks: not having fully waterproof plumage, it must dry off in the sun after each fishing session.

#### **Distribution and Habitat**

**Global Distribution**
Nearly cosmopolitan.

**Preferred Habitat**
Sea coasts, estuaries, inland lakes, large rivers. Anywhere there is water and fish.

**Presence in Sicily**
Very common winter visitor and localized breeder.

**Status in the Salt Pans**
Common especially in winter. Uses poles from old salt pans and abandoned mills as perches to rest and dry off.

**Morphological Characteristics**
Large black bird (80-100 cm).
Black plumage with bronze/bluish reflections. In breeding season has a white patch on the thigh and white cheeks.
Robust beak hooked at the tip. Emerald green eye.
In water it swims with the body deeply submerged, keeping only neck and head above.

**Behavior**
Hunts chasing fish underwater, propelled by its large webbed feet. Gregarious, forms communal roosts on trees or cliffs that are whitened by guano.

**Diet**
Exclusively piscivorous (fish of various sizes).

**Reproduction**
Nests in colonies on cliffs or trees.

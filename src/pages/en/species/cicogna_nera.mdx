Unlike its white cousin, the Black Stork is shy, solitary and tied to forest environments. It is an elegant and mysterious bird, difficult to observe except during migration.

#### **Distribution and Habitat**

**Global Distribution**
Palearctic and southern Africa.

**Preferred Habitat**
Large forests with streams or quiet ponds. Avoids human-modified areas.

**Presence in Sicily**
Regular migrant and irregular winter visitor.

**Status in the Salt Pans**
Occasional visitor during migratory passages, briefly stopping in the quietest areas of the salt pans or Stagnone to rest.

**Morphological Characteristics**
Similar size to the white stork but slightly slimmer.
Upper parts, head and neck black with strong green and violet iridescences. White belly.
Beak and legs scarlet red (more faded in juveniles).

**Behavior**
Solitary. Flies with neck extended. Very wary.

**Diet**
Fish, amphibians and aquatic insects caught in shallow forest waters.

**Reproduction**
Nests on large trees deep in the woods, away from humans.

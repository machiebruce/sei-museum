The Eurasian Golden Oriole is a touch of the tropics in our woodlands. The male is a dazzling yellow, but despite its bright colors it is incredibly difficult to spot among the high tree foliage.

#### **Distribution and Habitat**

**Global Distribution**
Europe and western Asia. Winters in Africa.

**Preferred Habitat**
Deciduous woodlands (poplar groves, oak forests), parks, orchards. Requires tall trees.

**Presence in Sicily**
Summer migrant and breeding.

**Status in the Salt Pans**
Passage visitor during migrations, stops in tall trees (eucalyptuses, pines) in the perimeter areas of the salt pans.

**Morphological Characteristics**
Size of a blackbird (24 cm).
Male: unmistakable **intense golden-yellow body**, black wings and tail. Reddish-pink beak.
Female and juveniles: olive yellowish-green above, pale striped belly (very camouflaged).

**Behavior**
Very shy and elusive, always stays in the dense canopy. Its song is a flutelike and powerful whistle "ui-a-uio," very melodious.

**Diet**
Fruit (cherries, figs, mulberries) and insects.

**Reproduction**
Builds a "hammock" nest suspended at the fork of a high horizontal branch.

The Eurasian Wigeon is a social and gregarious duck, known for the musical whistle of the male which is often heard in winter wetlands. It is a "grazing" duck that likes to browse grass on land.

#### **Distribution and Habitat**

**Global Distribution**
Breeds in northern Europe and Siberia (taiga). Winters in central and southern Europe.

**Preferred Habitat**
Flooded grasslands, salt pans, coastal lagoons with low vegetation.

**Presence in Sicily**
Common winter visitor and migrant.

**Status in the Salt Pans**
Frequents the salt pans in winter, often in mixed groups with other ducks, grazing on the banks or filtering water in the basins.

**Morphological Characteristics**
Medium-sized duck (45-50 cm), round head and small gray-blue beak.
Male: chestnut-colored head with a characteristic **cream-yellow forehead**, pinkish chest, gray body.
Female: variable reddish-brown.
In flight shows a conspicuous white patch on the wing (in the male).

**Behavior**
Gregarious. The male emits a sharp whistle "vii-u", very distinctive. Often grazes outside the water like geese.

**Diet**
Predominantly vegetarian: algae (e.g. *Ulva*), meadow grasses, seeds and aquatic plants (*Ruppia*).

**Reproduction**
Nests on the ground in boreal areas, hidden in tall grass or under bushes.

A mysterious nocturnal bird, the European Nightjar owes its name to an ancient legend claiming it sucked milk from goats (false: it approached herds only to eat the insects attracted by the animals). A master of camouflage.

#### **Distribution and Habitat**

**Global Distribution**
Palearctic.

**Preferred Habitat**
Heathlands, clearings, Mediterranean scrub, areas with scattered trees and bare ground.

**Presence in Sicily**
Summer migrant and breeder.

**Status in the Salt Pans**
Frequents uncultivated areas and coastal garigue on the edges of the salt pans. Becomes active at dusk hunting moths.

**Morphological Characteristics**
Medium-sized (26-28 cm).
**Cryptic "bark-like" plumage** (grey, brown, black) that renders it invisible when perched on the ground or along a branch.
Flat head, large black eyes, very small bill but enormous mouth surrounded by sensitive bristles for catching insects in flight.

**Behavior**
Crepuscular and nocturnal. Its song is a continuous mechanical buzz ("trrrrt...") that can last minutes, similar to the sound of a distant motor.

**Diet**
Moths and large nocturnal insects caught in flight with its mouth wide open.

**Reproduction**
Nests on the ground, without building a nest (lays two eggs directly on the soil).

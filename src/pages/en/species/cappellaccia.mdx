Similar to the Skylark but with a more "punk" appearance thanks to its always-conspicuous pointed crest, the Crested Lark is a resident species typical of arid, sunny Mediterranean environments.

#### **Distribution and Habitat**

**Global Distribution**
Temperate and southern Europe, northern Africa, Asia.

**Preferred Habitat**
Open arid areas, fallow land, roadsides, disused industrial areas, coastal dunes. More tolerant than the Skylark of anthropized environments.

**Presence in Sicily**
Very common resident.

**Status in the Salt Pans**
It is the easiest Alaudid species to encounter along the dirt paths and banks of the salt pans, often running in front of visitors.

**Morphological Characteristics**
Slightly larger than the Skylark (17-19 cm) and more robust.
Plumage sandy/grey-brown in color (less streaked and more uniform than the Skylark).
Distinctive feature: **long pointed crest** always erect on the head. Slightly curved beak. Undertail coverts isabelline (not pure white).

**Behavior**
Very confiding. Often sings perched on walls, posts or piles of earth, emitting a melodious and melancholy whistle.

**Feeding**
Seeds, insects, food scraps.

**Reproduction**
Nests on the ground.

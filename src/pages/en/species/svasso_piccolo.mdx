The Black-necked Grebe is a graceful and sociable waterbird, known for its ruby-red eye shining against the dark plumage. In winter it often forms large groups ("rafts") at sea or in salt pans.

#### **Distribution and Habitat**

**Global Distribution**
Europe, Asia, Africa, North America.

**Preferred Habitat**
Freshwater wetlands rich in vegetation for nesting. In winter it moves to brackish lagoons, salt pans and sheltered marine coasts.

**Presence in Sicily**
Common winter visitor and migrant. Occasional/localized breeding.

**Status in the Salt Pans**
Very common in winter in the deeper basins of the salt pans, where it finds calm waters rich in invertebrates.

**Morphological Characteristics**
Small (28-34 cm), slightly larger than the Little Grebe.
High forehead and thin bill slightly upturned.
Summer: black neck and head with **fan-shaped golden-yellow ear tufts** behind the eye. Chestnut flanks.
Winter: white and black/grey (the black of the cap extends below the eye, unlike the Great Crested Grebe). **Intense red eye** always evident.

**Behavior**
Excellent swimmer and diver. Very gregarious in winter.

**Diet**
Aquatic invertebrates, larvae, small crustaceans.

**Reproduction**
Nests in colonies on floating nests.

Famous for its call ("cuck-oo") that announces spring and for its brood parasitism behavior, the Common Cuckoo is a bird that does not build a nest but lays its eggs in the nests of others.

#### **Distribution and Habitat**

**Global Distribution**
Palearctic. Winters in tropical Africa.

**Preferred Habitat**
Woods, wooded countryside, reed beds (where it often parasitizes the Reed Warbler).

**Presence in Sicily**
Migrant and breeding.

**Status in the Salt Pans**
Observed (and especially heard) in nearby wooded areas or perched on poles along reed beds, searching for passerine nests to parasitize.

**Morphological Characteristics**
Size of a turtle dove (32-34 cm), but slender silhouette similar to a sparrowhawk.
Gray plumage on top, white belly barred with black (similar to raptors, to scare small birds).
Long tail, pointed wings. A "rufous" form exists (rust-colored females).

**Behavior**
Solitary. The female lays an egg in the host's nest (e.g. Blackcap, Reed Warbler), removing an original egg. The cuckoo chick hatches first and pushes the other eggs or legitimate chicks out of the nest, then is fed by the unsuspecting foster parents.

**Diet**
Insects, particularly hairy caterpillars (which other birds avoid).

**Reproduction**
Obligate brood parasite.

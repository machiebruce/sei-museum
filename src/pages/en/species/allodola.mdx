The Eurasian Skylark is famous for its melodious, incessant song performed in flight at high altitude. It is the soul of open fields and traditional agricultural areas.

#### **Distribution and Habitat**

**Global Distribution**
Palearctic (Europe, Asia, North Africa).

**Preferred Habitat**
Open countryside, pastures, extensive croplands (wheat, alfalfa), dune back zones. Avoids wooded areas.

**Presence in Sicily**
Sedentary and winter migrant.

**Status in the Salt Pans**
It frequents the wider grassy banks and neighboring agricultural areas (Pre Reserve), where it nests on the ground.

**Morphological Characteristics**
Small passerine (18-19 cm).
Brown streaked plumage, very cryptic.
It has a small erectile crest on the head (less evident than in the Crested Lark). Tail with white edges visible in flight.

**Behavior**
Terrestrial. If disturbed, it flattens against the ground relying on its camouflage. The male climbs vertically while singing until it becomes almost invisible in the sky, then parachutes down.

**Feeding**
Seeds, shoots and insects (especially during the breeding period).

**Reproduction**
Nests on the ground, hidden among the grass.

IUCN: LC (but declining in Europe).

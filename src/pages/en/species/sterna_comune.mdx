Also known as the "Sea Swallow" for its elegance and forked tail, the Common Tern is an agile fisher that defends its nest with extreme courage, even attacking much larger intruders.

#### **Distribution and Habitat**

**Global Distribution**
Wide range in the northern hemisphere (North America, Europe, Asia). Winters in the southern hemisphere.

**Preferred Habitat**
Islands, beaches, salt pans and inland wetlands. Less tied to the open sea than other terns.

**Presence in Sicily**
Migrant and breeder.

**Status in the Salt Pans**
Often seen resting on poles or wooden structures inside the basins, or diving into the channels.

**Morphological Characteristics**
Medium-sized (31-35 cm).
Streamlined white/pearl grey body, full black cap extending to the nape.
The **bill is coral red with a black tip** (in summer) and the legs are red. Deeply forked tail.

**Behavior**
Graceful and light flight. During courtship, the male offers small fish to the female ("fish-flight").

**Diet**
Mainly fish, caught by diving.

**Reproduction**
Nests in dense colonies. The nest is a depression in the ground, sometimes lined with shells or vegetation.

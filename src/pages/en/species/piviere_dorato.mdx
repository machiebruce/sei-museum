The European Golden Plover is a splendid wader, whose plumage seems sprinkled with gold. In winter it often gathers in large, compact flocks in cultivated fields and wetlands.

#### **Distribution and Habitat**

**Global Distribution**
Northern Europe and Siberia. Winters in western Europe and the Mediterranean.

**Preferred Habitat**
Heathlands and tundra for breeding. In winter: meadows, ploughed fields, pastures and salt pans.

**Presence in Sicily**
Wintering and migratory.

**Status in the Salt Pans**
Frequents the more open and dry areas of the salt pans and the surrounding fields (Pre-reserve), often in association with Lapwings.

**Morphological Characteristics**
Robust wader (26-29 cm), round head.
Upperparts densely speckled with **gold, black and white**.
Winter: face and underparts pale/beige.
Summer: face, throat and belly black bordered with white, in strong contrast with the golden back.
Axillaries (underwing) **white** (key distinction from the Grey Plover which has black axillaries).

**Behavior**
Fast and direct flight. Gregarious. Emits a melancholic, liquid whistle.

**Diet**
Earthworms, insects, snails, berries. Hunts by sight, running and stopping.

**Reproduction**
Nests on the ground in heathlands.

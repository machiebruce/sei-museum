Often described as a "flying jewel" for its iridescent colors, the Common Kingfisher is an indicator of good environmental quality. Despite its bright colors, it is hard to spot until it streaks away just above the water like a blue bolt.

#### **Distribution and Habitat**

**Global Distribution**
Europe, North Africa and Asia.

**Preferred Habitat**
Clear fresh or brackish waters (rivers, canals, lagoons) with small fish and perches (branches, reeds) overhanging the water.

**Presence in Sicily**
Resident and wintering.

**Status in the Salt Pans**
It frequents the perimeter canals and pools with clear water. It often uses wooden sluice gates as hunting perches.

**Morphological Characteristics**
Small and compact (16-17 cm), large head and very short tail.
Back and wings **iridescent turquoise blue/cobalt blue**. Underparts and cheek **rusty orange**. White throat and neck patch.
Long dagger-like beak: entirely black in the male, with the lower mandible orange in the female (as if wearing lipstick).

**Behavior**
Hunts by ambush from a perch or by hovering. It dives like lightning to catch prey and returns to the perch to stun it by beating it against the wood before swallowing (head first).

**Diet**
Small fish, aquatic insect larvae, shrimps.

**Reproduction**
Digs a tunnel up to a meter long in vertical sandy or earthen riverbanks.

**Conservation Status**
IUCN: LC.

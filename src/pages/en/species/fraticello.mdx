The Little Tern is the smallest of the European terns. Its name ("little friar") probably comes from the black cap on its head that resembles a tonsure. It is a frantic and lively bird, capable of spectacular dives to catch fish.

#### **Distribution and Habitat**

**Global Distribution**
Cosmopolitan species, breeds in scattered colonies along the coasts of temperate and Mediterranean Europe, Africa and Asia.

**Preferred Habitat**
Sandy or gravel-pebble beaches, salt pans, coastal lagoons and occasionally large rivers. Requires bare ground or ground with sparse vegetation to lay eggs.

**Presence in Sicily**
Summer migrant and regular breeder. Arrives in April and departs in September for West Africa.

**Status in the Salt Pans**
Regularly nests on the embankments and islets of the Trapani Salt Pans. Its sharp call is a typical soundtrack of summer in the salt pan.

**Morphological Characteristics**
Small size (20-25 cm).
White and pale grey plumage. The head has a black cap with a **white forehead** (distinctive feature).
The bill is yellow with a black tip; legs are yellow-orange. In flight it is very fast, with rapid wingbeats.

**Behavior**
Hunts by hovering ("spirito santo" style) before diving vertically into water to catch small fish.

**Diet**
Small surface fish and crustaceans.

**Reproduction**
Nests in small colonies on bare ground, often on beaches or salt pan embankments. The eggs (2-3) are incredibly camouflaged, looking like stones.
